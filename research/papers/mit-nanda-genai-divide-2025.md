# MIT NANDA — The GenAI Divide

*Stub paper-summary. Full version lands in v1.x.*

## Headline findings (cited in MANIFESTO Thesis 1)

- **95% of enterprise GenAI pilots produce no measurable P&L impact.** Only 5% achieve rapid revenue acceleration.
- The root cause of failure is a **learning gap** — organisational and process-level, not model quality.
- **External tools succeed roughly twice as often as internal builds** (~67% vs. ~33%).
- **90% of workers use shadow AI tools daily**; only 40% of companies have official LLM subscriptions.

## Why ØØT cites it

This is the empirical foundation for Thesis 1: *Resistance is the central problem.* The framework's Skill Pack S6 (Change Management) and the mandatory METR baseline discipline are the framework's response to the 95% failure pattern.

## Method

Survey of enterprise pilots (n>500); follow-up interviews with leaders of high-performing 5%; cross-referenced with workforce surveys.

## Limitations

- Self-reported pilot status (high-performing leaders may overstate; failed pilots may understate).
- US-skewed sample.
- "P&L impact" definition varies across firms.
