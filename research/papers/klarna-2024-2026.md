# Klarna 2024-2026 — the canonical cautionary tale

*Stub paper-summary. Full version lands in v1.x.*

## Timeline

- **2024:** Klarna publicly cuts ~700 customer-service roles. CEO Sebastian Siemiatkowski credits AI for the saving. Extensive favourable coverage of Klarna as a leading example of AI-driven workforce transformation.
- **2025:** customer satisfaction degrades materially. Complaint resolution times lengthen.
- **2025-2026:** Klarna quietly rehires. Adopts a hybrid "Uber-style" workforce model with on-demand human agents brought back to handle work the AI assistant could not credibly do alone.
- **Headcount trajectory:** ~5,500 (start of 2024) → ~3,400 (early 2026). Net cut ~40% over the period; the headline AI-replaces-humans narrative collapsed publicly.

## Why ØØT cites it

The Klarna story is the **namesake** of the framework's Klarna Test. The test exists because every organisation considering AI-driven workforce changes will believe they are the exception to Klarna. Sometimes they will be right. Often they will be wrong. The cost of being wrong is not just the rehiring — it is the trust loss with remaining staff, the public credibility hit, and the structural difficulty of running a hybrid restoration model after a clean-cut announcement.

The lesson the framework codifies: **don't automate before quality bars are proven and reversal plans are real.**
