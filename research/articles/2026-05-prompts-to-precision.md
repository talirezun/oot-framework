## Summary

*A condensed summary will land here in v1.x. The full article is at the external link above.*

The original ØØT formulation of the **five-step prompt skeleton** — Goal / Context / Constraints / Format / Self-Check — and the argument for why every partner-facing prompt in the firm's Brain should follow it. The skeleton makes prompts diffable, reviewable, and reusable across Skill Packs. It is the foundation of Skill Pack S2 (Context Engineering) and shows up in the worked examples for every Tier-1 pack that drafts customer-facing or partner-facing output.

## Why this matters for ØØT

S2 is the connective tissue of the cloud and privacy tracks. Without a stable prompt discipline, every Skill Pack ends up reinventing the wheel and the firm's outputs lose comparability. The five-step skeleton is what makes the Klarna Test enforceable on AI-authored work — you can audit a prompt the same way you audit a commit.
