# Partner Onboarding — First 90 Days

A week-by-week plan for a new partner's first 90 days. The plan assumes the cloud track; privacy-track partners follow the same plan with track-specific tools (Trezor + 4thtech + PollinationX in place of Slack + Google Workspace).

This is a **template**. The founder customises per partner — a senior engineering hire's first 90 days will look different from a sales specialist's. The structural backbone (week-1 onboarding session, weekly BR participation, 30-day and 90-day check-ins) is non-negotiable.

---

## Week 1 — orientation

### Day 1 (Monday)

- **90-minute onboarding session** (founder + partner). Checklist steps **1-15** run in the session itself.
- **Tools provisioned** by end of day. Checklist steps **16-17** (Curator self-test + welcome announcement) complete end-of-day, right after the session.
- **Welcome to the partnership** announcement in `#general` (step 17).

### Day 2 — Day 5

- **Read** the foundation kit (Manifesto, SPEC, Generations, Klarna Test, Decision Rights, Secrets Policy, glossary). 2-3 hours each across the week.
- **Read** the Skill Packs relevant to the partner's role (S1 + S2 + S3 universal; S4 if engineering, S11 if sales, etc.).
- **Begin work** on the first Output Spec drafted on Day 1. R1 captures the first commits/contracts/etc. that evening.
- **Daily check-in** with founder (15 min) for the first 5 days. Friction points surfaced; not problems, just field reports.

### Day 5 (Friday)

- **Attend the Friday BR.** Listen. Don't speak much. Read the Ledger summary after the meeting.
- **End-of-week reflection** committed to the partner's `private/` Brain folder. What surprised them. What was hard. What was easier than expected.

---

## Weeks 2-3 — first ledger entries

- **Daily output capture** is now real. R1 captures the partner's commits/contracts/deals/etc. The partner reviews their `firm/output-logs/<date>.md` page each evening.
- **First Output Spec ships** (or progress visible). The partner drafts the second Output Spec in collaboration with the founder.
- **Friday BR participation increases.** Partner can ask questions, raise blockers, contribute to discussions. Decisions still flow through the Accountable per the RACI matrix.
- **First 1-1 with founder** at end of week 2 (30 min). Less formal than 30-day check-in. Topic: how is the work going? Are the outputs being captured correctly? Any concerns about the Reward Species Declaration?

---

## Weeks 4-5 — first month closes

- **R3 fires on the 1st of the next month.** First variable pay statement lands at `firm/partners/<partner_id>/variable-statements/<month>.md`.
- **The partner reads the statement.** They tick the agree box (most cases) or the dispute box (Tier-1 dispute flow). Either way, they engage with the calculation.
- **First Tier-1 dispute (if any).** The framework's discipline: disputes are normal, not problematic. The first month often surfaces value-tier classification disagreements that wouldn't have been visible without the daily ledger.
- **30-day check-in** (steps 26-30 of the onboarding checklist). Founder + partner review the first month's data, refine the Reward Species Declaration if needed, confirm the Two Worlds of Code self-id is still accurate.

---

## Weeks 6-8 — building rhythm

- **Daily ledger reviews** become routine. The partner spots their own value-tier mis-classifications before R3 fires.
- **Brain contributions broaden.** The partner is now drafting Output Specs without much founder hand-holding; contributing to ADRs or decision records as relevant; helping other partners on tooling questions (the AI Champion criterion #3 is starting to apply if the partner is uptake-active).
- **Skill Pack mastery deepens.** The partner is now applying the Skill Packs reliably — the five-step prompt skeleton (S2), the Plan Mode workflow (S4 if engineering), the Brain ontology (S1) — without consulting the SKILL.md every time.

---

## Weeks 9-12 — first quarterly cycle

- **R4 fires on quarter open.** First long-tail settlement (if any of the partner's outputs were long-tail-eligible). Quarterly statement at `firm/partners/<partner_id>/long-tail-statements/<quarter>.md`.
- **Quarterly partner check-in (S5 §4.6).** 45-60 min with founder. Covers: variable pay trend, long-tail growth, reward-species declared vs. observed cohort behaviour, any disputes, Klarna Test exposure, AI Champion candidacy signals, framework feedback.
- **The partner is now a full participant in the partnership.** They take their turn as facilitator or scribe at the Friday BR. They participate in decisions per the RACI matrix.

### Day 90 — formal onboarding-complete review

The 90-day check-in. The partner is "fully onboarded" only after this review confirms:

1. The partner is producing output reliably at the expected cadence + value tier.
2. The variable pay calculations are uncontested (or disputes are well-handled).
3. The partner is participating in the Friday BR + cross-partner work.
4. The Brain has the partner's profile, output specs, ledger entries, and any contributions to architecture/decisions.
5. The partner reports they would recommend the framework to a peer.

If any of these is `No`, the founder + partner discuss what's needed. The partnership continues; the formal "onboarding complete" tick is delayed until all five hold.

---

## What the founder watches for

- **Week 1:** does the partner ask questions? Silence in week 1 is a warning sign. The framework is dense; questions are expected.
- **Weeks 2-3:** is the partner's output mix consistent with their cohort designation? A "full-time partner" producing only XS-tier outputs week after week is either mis-cohorted or mis-supported.
- **Weeks 4-5:** does the partner engage with their first variable pay statement? Auto-acknowledgement without inspection is a warning sign.
- **Weeks 6-8:** is the partner contributing to the Brain beyond their own outputs? Cross-partner help, decision contributions, prompt artefacts shared in `firm/prompts/`.
- **Weeks 9-12:** is the quarterly check-in productive? Does the partner have framework feedback? A partner who sails through 90 days with zero feedback is either remarkably well-fitted (rare) or not engaging (common).

---

## What the partner watches for

- **Their own velocity vs. expectation.** Pace concerns surface early; better to flag at week 4 than week 12.
- **Reward species fit.** The first month's variable pay reveals whether the declared species matches their actual work pattern. Renegotiation is built in (per Charter §6 + S3 §4.1 renegotiation flow); use it if needed.
- **Cross-partner relationships.** The first 90 days are the critical window for forming the working relationships that will sustain over years.
- **Tools fit.** Privacy track in particular — does the always-on machine work for the partner? Are they comfortable with 4thtech / dChat? If not, surface early.

---

## When 90 days is too aggressive

For some partners and some firms, 90 days is too short. Specifically:
- **Project specialists** with 4-6 week engagements — the 30-day check-in is the substantive review; 90-day is replaced by project-end review.
- **Advisors** — the framework's onboarding is lighter; full integration into the daily ledger is not expected.
- **Highly senior partners** taking on a leadership cohort designation — the first 90 days may be more about strategic alignment than ledger entries.

The founder customises the plan. The structural backbone (90-min onboarding session, daily-week-1 check-ins, Friday BR participation, 30-day and 90-day reviews) is non-negotiable; the contents within each milestone are negotiable.
