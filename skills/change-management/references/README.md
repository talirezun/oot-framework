# References — Skill Pack S6 (Change Management / Resistance)

The intellectual and empirical foundations of the framework's response to its central problem.

## Primary sources

1. **Kotter, J. P.** *Leading Change* (Harvard Business School Press, 1996, updated editions). The eight-step framework the pack borrows from.

2. **Hiatt, J.** *ADKAR: A Model for Change in Business, Government, and our Community* (Prosci, 2006). The ADKAR five-stage model.

3. **METR.** *RCT on AI tools and developer productivity* (July 2025). https://metr.org/blog/2025-07-10-early-2025-ai-experienced-os-dev-study/. The 39-point perception-gap study. Plus February 2026 follow-up confirming the structural pattern. The single most-cited paper in the framework.

## Empirical evidence base

4. **MIT NANDA.** *The GenAI Divide* (August 2025). https://mlq.ai/media/quarterly_decks/v0.1_State_of_AI_in_Business_2025_Report.pdf. The 95% pilot-failure finding. The 67% external-vs-33% internal success rate. Source for the "build pilots that don't fail" discipline.

5. **Microsoft.** *Work Trend Index 2025: The Frontier Firm.* https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/worklab/work-trend-index/2025-the-year-the-frontier-firm-is-born. The 82% / 81% / 46% framing, the human-agent ratio.

6. **Forsgren, N., Humble, J., Kim, G.** *Accelerate* (IT Revolution Press, 2018) + **DORA Report 2025: State of AI-Assisted Software Development.** The DORA metrics the §4.1 baseline procedure uses.

7. **Dell'Acqua, F. et al.** *The Cybernetic Teammate* (HBS WP 25-043, 2025). https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=5188231. The pilot-cohort composition framing (70% participants, 20% control, 10% champion-candidates) is informed by this work's methodology.

## AI Champion case studies

8. **Tecknoworks / Caplaz.** *AI Champion case studies* (2024–2025). Industry case studies showing the appointed-vs-recognised distinction and the failure mode of premature appointment. The framework's "recognised, not appointed" discipline derives from these case studies.

## Adjacent literature

9. **Schein, E.** *Organizational Culture and Leadership* (4th ed., Jossey-Bass, 2010). Cultural dimensions of resistance to change.

10. **Edmondson, A.** *The Fearless Organization* (Wiley, 2018). Psychological safety in pilots; informs the "consent-first" cohort selection discipline.

11. **Christensen, C. M.** *The Innovator's Dilemma* (HBR Press, 1997). For why the discipline of structured pilots-and-rollbacks matters: organisations fail at innovation precisely when they bypass the discipline.
